You are feeling angry today.

Anger can be an overwhelming emotion, but it can also help ourselves deal with uncomfortable situations and external pressures. Despite it's negative effects at times, anger is energizing, communicates to others how you are feeling, and stops intense, dangerous feelings from taking over.


Your heart rate might increase, you might yell, you might feel like crying, and you might feel like hurting someone when you're angry. It's important to safely manage these feelings and take safe steps to treat yourself and others with kindness.


To manage anger, try taking a few deep breaths, or counting down from 10 slowly in your head. You can also go for a run, listen to your favorite music, or drink a large glass of water to help calm down.


Sometimes, anger is a sign of a larger emotional or social issue. You might feel angry when you are very very sad, or when something negative feels like it's taking over your life. No matter the reason why you are angry, you can always find support for your feelings.
